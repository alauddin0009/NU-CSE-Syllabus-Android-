
<h2><strong>GED-315 Sociology</strong></h2>
<p>3 hours in a week, 3.00 Cr.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Sociological perspective:</strong> definition, nature, scope and importance of sociology.</p>
<p><strong>Sociology and scientific approaches:</strong> methods of social research, stages of social research.</p>
<p><strong>Primary concepts of sociology:</strong> society, community, association, institution, group.</p>
<p><strong>Social evaluation:</strong> stages in the evolution of human civilization.</p>
<p><strong>Culture:</strong> definition, characteristics, culture content (material and non-material), cultural lag, culture and civilization.</p>
<p><strong>Industrial revolution:</strong> The growth of capitalism, features and social consequences, socialism.</p>
<p><strong>Social organization: </strong>family, forms and functions of family, functions of family in modern industrial society, marriage, forms of marriage, functions of marriage.</p>
<p><strong>Social stratification: </strong>main types of social stratification- slavery-caste and social class and status, social stratification and social mobility</p>
<p><strong>Social control: </strong>religion and morality, custom and public opinion, taboo-low, state and education.</p>
<p><strong>Social change:</strong> change evolution-progress-development, factors in social change.</p>
<p><strong>Society and population:</strong> human migration, population and resources.</p>
<p><strong>Some current social</strong> <strong>problems:</strong> crime, deviance, juvenile delinquency, youth unrest.</p>
<p>Te<strong>chnology and society:</strong> effects of technological factors on social life.</p>
